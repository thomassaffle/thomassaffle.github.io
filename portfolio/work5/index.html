The Protestant Reformation online exhibition site

Responsive Website Design / Information Architecture

Project Goal and challenges

To create a comprehensive digital exhibit in Omeka CMS (an open source Content Management System) that highlights University of Arizona Special Collections materials associated with Martin Luther, the Protestant Reformation, and related historical contexts. I was a designer who is responsible for providing UX support and visual design.

During this project, besides considering responsiveness, usability, and accessibility for the site, I was also met with these other challenges.

  • How to present the historical materials from Specoll (Special Collections) successfully.
  • How to manage the site’s extensive content.
  • How to work with Omeka CMS to create a visually compelling site.

Homepage of The Protestant Reformation online exhibition site

With user experience in mind, in order to accommodate the vast amount of content, I decided to use a side-slide navigation bar instead of a fixed menu. We didn’t choose a horizontal navigation bar because, in the future, Specoll may want to add more pages. A vertical menu bar has more flexibility; it’s easier to add buttons without breaking the design. Also, a side-slide menu supports the page responsively.

To improve the onsite reading experience, I increased the font size and chose an easy-to-read font: Merriweather. Adjusting the background and font colors to reduce color contrast also made it easier to read.

I also added a new content type: Pull quote, a short initial paragraph that summarized the site’s theme and put it at the top of the page, so that readers can have a basic idea of what the entire site is about without reading everything.

I added bottom navigation so that users can easily move to previous pages or subsequent pages without having to click on the menu button at the top of each page.

Universal Book Reader

There were several lengthy pamphlets in the Specoll’s Reformation collection that we want to show, the longest consisting of 772 pages. The current Omeka system doesn’t effectively present this kind of material well. After a three-day research spike with developers and a content strategist, we decided to add a universal book reader plugin. With a universal book reader, users can read the collections much more easily. Also, because of its lazy load function, even books with over 700 pages and scanned in high resolution, the site loading speed remained fast.
